Allepey

Alappuzha (Alleppey) is known as ‘the Venice of the East’. Offering the best tourist places in Alleppey, this charming place is the hub of Kerala’s backwaters and is home to a huge network of canals and lagoons with backwaters and more than thousand houseboats offering overnight stays. The houseboats you find in the backwaters of Alappuzha are in fact a reworked version of the Kettuvallams of olden times.

Alleppey Backwaters


The Alleppey backwaters are extended to approx 300 sq km. To visit most beautiful places in Aleppey backwaters which include the scenic place like Kuttanad, Kainakery canals, minimum 2 days in a houseboat is required. But short trip of 3-4 hrs in small canals is also possible to see the glimpse of Alleppey canals.

Every route has something unique and captivates you more than the other. Alappuzha to Kumarakom is considered one of the most beautiful backwater routes. This one night cruise makes you enjoy a magical night under the starry sky.

From sunset viewing, bird watching, stargazing, visiting villages and witnessing the everyday life of the farmers and fishermen to seeing the various regions that specialize in the manufacturing of certain products like toddy, coir and unique handicrafts, Alleppey backwaters is one of the most pleasant experiences to have once in a lifetime.

Best time to visit is during Winters (October to February)




Houseboat Cruises


Alleppey is famous for its luxurious houseboat cruises that allow visitors to enjoy a floating experience amidst the lush greenery and tranquil backwaters. Offers an overnight stay on houseboats with all modern amenities and traditional Kerala cuisine.

Alleppey houseboat rates start from INR 5k and goes as high at INR 50k and even more. The Alleppey houseboat packages also include sightseeing trip of the city, visit to Kottayam and trip to Kumarakom backwaters..

Lakkidi View Point is about 20 km from Wayanad, on the way to Kozhikode.

Best Time to visit is- November to February
Visiting Time:Early Morning and early Evening





Light House


The Alappuzha beach, also known as the Alleppey Beach, is famous for local getaways, its intrinsic beauty and a 150 years old pier which stretches into the sea.Alleppey Beach is a host to many festivals like Sand Art festival and Alappuzha Beach Festival

Known for its breathtaking 360-degree views, the Alappuzha Lighthouse(Alleppey lighthouse) is an old-world charm. It is 4 km away from the main town lying on the sea bridge facing the Arabian sea coast. The 1862 lighthouse has a museum that preserves old equipment like oil lamps and items that testify to its 150-year-old journey.

In addition to the pristine beauty of Alleppey Beach, some exciting activities like water spots, yoga and meditation are present in and around this destination.
